# Mindset

The strategy assumes a temperament, not just a method. Knowledge without the right
mindset tends to fail — plenty of finance professionals lose money personally because
they get emotional with their own capital.

Five principles the framework leans on:

1. **Have your own principle and stick to it.** Every strategy works for *someone*;
   none works for everyone. Match the approach to your own character — Macropulse
   assumes a mid-to-long-term, valuation-driven, macro-timed investor.
2. **Don't get swayed by noise.** The moment you chase someone else's win you become
   a follower dragged into the game rather than a decision-maker.
3. **Review your principle regularly.** Markets evolve; test and adapt with your own
   logic, not by copying others.
4. **Remove emotional attachment after you invest.** Treat invested money as already
   committed. Checking a long-term position many times a day is a red flag.
5. **Keep your positions to yourself.** Broadcasting what you bought creates anxiety
   that erodes discipline.

These are heuristics, not rules — adapt them to fit how you actually operate.
